Understanding Learning Disabilities Through Technology

For a long time, I noticed how misunderstood learning disabilities are. Many students feel embarrassed to use the learning center or ask for extra help because of societal stigma.

These assumptions exist because schools often fail to teach what learning disabilities are and what the experience of learning with one is actually like. As a result, students who do not face these challenges may struggle to empathize with the difficulties that students with learning disabilities experience.

This lack of understanding can lead to students being made fun of or having their hard work discredited because of accommodations such as extra time. I have experienced this firsthand since middle school, when I was diagnosed with ADHD and a learning disability in reading, and it has continued to affect me throughout high school. Watching my peers easily focus or effortlessly read passages has impacted my self-esteem and confidence in my academic abilities. When teachers asked if everyone had finished reading, I often raised my hand just to blend in, even when I had not. Moments like these showed me how invisible learning disabilities can feel and how easily they are misunderstood by others.

Through this project, I hope to create a resource that helps students, educators, and anyone interested in learning disabilities to better understand these conditions. By providing interactive simulations alongside informative content, I aim to foster empathy and awareness about the challenges faced by individuals with learning disabilities.
