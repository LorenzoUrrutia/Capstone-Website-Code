Comprehensive Evaluation

A comprehensive ADHD assessment is designed to improve diagnostic accuracy and create a clear treatment plan.

① Multi-step process

Conducted by a psychologist, psychiatrist, or neuropsychologist across multiple sessions totaling 3-6 hours of assessment.

② Interviews & rating scales

In-depth interviews with the patient, parents, and teachers are combined with standardized questionnaires to measure symptom severity.

③ Cognitive testing

Measures working memory, processing speed, attention, and executive function to build a full picture of how the brain is performing.

④ Rules out other conditions

Anxiety, depression, sleep disorders, and learning disabilities can all mimic ADHD and must be eliminated before a diagnosis is confirmed.

⑤ Why it matters

A short screening alone can lead to misdiagnosis; a comprehensive evaluation ensures accuracy and provides specific recommendations for treatment and support.

Diagnostic Criteria Highlights

  1. A person must have at least 5-6 symptoms occur frequently.
  2. Several symptoms must have been present before the age 12.
  3. Symptoms must be noticeably greater than expected for the person's age.
  4. Symptoms must appear in more than one setting, such as at school and at home.
