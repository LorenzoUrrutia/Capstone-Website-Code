Reflection

The moving and shifting text in this simulation is designed to approximate the feeling of visual instability that some people with dyslexia experience. If you tried the higher intensity, you may have noticed letters swapping places and words blurring together. People with dyslexia face these challenges every day, every book, every test, every moment they are required to read. And unlike you, they cannot close the tab and walk away from it. Over time that kind of daily struggle builds into something deeper. A nervousness is created around reading out loud, a fear of looking slow in front of others, an anxiety that follows them into every classroom they walk into. It is important to note that in reality the text on a page does not physically move, but difficulties with visual processing and letter recognition can make it feel exactly like it does. This is only a simplified representation, and real experiences with dyslexia can vary widely.
