ADHD Dyslexia Dyscalculia Simulations

Reflection

The distractions and interruptions in this simulation are designed to approximate how attention can be pulled away or become difficult to maintain. In real life, these challenges come from internal attention regulation differences and everyday environments, not constant popups like those shown here. This simulation is designed to overload your working memory, forcing you to hold onto information you have already read while irrelevant information and new distractions continuously pull your attention away, recreating the exhausting mental juggle that people with ADHD experience every day. Imagine every time you try to complete a task, you are constantly distracted and forget important information, making you start over. That is what people with ADHD experience for their whole lives. This is only a simplified representation, and experiences with ADHD can vary widely.
