What is Dyslexia?

  • Dyslexia is a lifelong reading related disorder that makes reading and language related tasks harder, primarily impacting a persons phonological processing: the ability to accurately and fluently recognize words, decode unfamiliar words, and spell
  • Dyslexia affects 7% of the population world-wide and occurs because of disruptions in the way the brain processes written and spoken language so you can understand it

Dyslexia Profiles

  • Dyslexia does not have official subtypes. Instead, it exists on a spectrum, meaning individuals can experience different challenges and levels of difficulty with reading and language
  • Some symptoms of Dyslexia include difficulty with accurate and/or fluent word recognition, poor spelling, and decoding abilities

Causes of Dyslexia

  • Dyslexia is a neurodevelopmental disorder caused by differences in how the brain develops and functions
  • Researchers have used brain-imaging scans to compare how the brain of a kid with and without dyslexia compare. These studies have found that kids with dyselxia show less activity in areas of the brain that process language
  • Dyslexia also runs in families. Researchers have found several genes that affect brain development and language skills

Diagnosis

  • Dyslexia is not diagnosed with a single test. Initial assesments involve short dyslexia screenings to flag a risk for dyselxia.
  • If the screener indicates a potential risk, a comprehensive evaluation by a specialist is recommended for a definitive diagnosis.
  • Dyslexia diagnosis is typically made when there is a significant gap between listening comprehension skills and word reading and spelling skills.
