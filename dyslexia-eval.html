Below is an Example of a Comprehensive Evaluation

A full dyslexia evaluation usually combines interviews, structured reading tasks, and a written report so families and schools can understand both strengths and support needs.

① Online meeting

First, a licensed therapist meets with the child to guide a sequence of reading tasks.

② Report and diagnosis

Then an 8-10 page report describes the test results of the meeting, any diagnosis, and suggested next steps, including recommended school accommodations.

③ Follow-up and action plan

Finally, a 30-minute follow-up with your therapist reviews findings, answers questions, and outlines practical next steps and treatment or support options.
