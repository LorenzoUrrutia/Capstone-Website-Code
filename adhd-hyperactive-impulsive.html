Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive Type

This subtype is marked by high activity and impulsive behavior. Individuals may move a lot, interrupt others, or act without thinking; they often appear restless and may struggle to sit still for expected periods.

Common signs

  • Fidgets with or taps hands or feet, or squirms in seat.
  • Not being able to remain seated when remaining seated is expected.
  • Runs about or climbs in situations where it is inappropriate.
  • Unable to play or participate in leisure activities quietly.
  • Talks excessively.
  • Blurts out an answer before a question has been completed or finishes other people's sentences.
  • Has difficulty waiting for their turn.
  • Interrupts or intrudes on others.

In school this can look like…

  • Leaving their seat during lessons.
  • Calling out answers without raising a hand.
  • Struggling to wait in line or during group activities.
