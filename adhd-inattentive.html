Predominantly Inattentive Type

This subtype primarily affects attention and the ability to sustain focus. People with the inattentive type often seem quiet or 'spacey', and they may miss details, lose track of tasks, or need extra time to complete reading or written work.

Common signs

  • Does not pay close attention to details or makes careless mistakes in schoolwork.
  • Has difficulty staying focused on tasks or activities.
  • Does not seem to listen when spoken to.
  • Often does not follow through on instructions and fails to finish schoolwork, chores, or duties in the workplace.
  • Avoiding tasks that require sustained mental effort.
  • Often loses things necessary for tasks or daily life.
  • Gets easily distracted by extraneous stimuli.
  • Forgets daily tasks.

In school this can look like…

  • Missing details on worksheets and tests.
  • Starting assignments but not finishing them.
  • Frequently asking the teacher to repeat instructions.
