Comprehensive Evaluation

A dyscalculia evaluation looks at number sense, math reasoning, and related skills so the final report can explain what is causing the difficulty and what support will help most.

① Intake interview

A specialist gathers background information on math history, development, and classroom performance.

② Standardized testing

The evaluator administers number-sense tasks, arithmetic fluency measures, and problem-solving tests, along with checks for attention, working memory, and language skills.

③ Report and action plan

A written report summarizes results, confirms or rules out dyscalculia, and recommends supports and accommodations.
