Below is an Example of a Comprehensive Evaluation

① Intake interview: A specialist gathers background information on math history, development, and classroom performance.

② Standardized testing: The evaluator administers number-sense tasks, arithmetic fluency measures, and problem-solving tests, along with checks for attention, working memory, and language skills.

③ Report and action plan: A written report summarizes results, confirms or rules out dyscalculia, and recommends supports and accommodations.
